### 2.6 Participant Image Submission Guide

---

#### Purpose

This document explains how participants submit their selected image each week directly to the project gallery, without routing through the facilitator. It is written for participants, not facilitators. It should be printed and handed out at the Phase 0 orientation alongside the consent forms.

A facilitator version with additional notes is included at the end of this document.

---

#### For Participants

**How to submit your image each week**

At the end of each group session your facilitator will show you a card with a QR code on it. This QR code opens a short form on your phone that lets you send your chosen image directly to the *Through Two Sets of Eyes* gallery.

You can submit your image at the session, or take a photo of the QR code card and submit later in the week when you've had more time to choose. Your image needs to be submitted before the next session so it can be printed for the exhibition.

**Step by step**

1. Open your phone's camera and point it at the QR code - most phones open the link automatically. If yours doesn't, look for a QR code scanner app or ask your facilitator for help.
2. The form opens in your phone's web browser. You don't need to download anything.
3. Check that the week number shown on the form is correct.
4. Enter your participant code - the personal code you created at the first session. If you've forgotten it, look at the code generation card you were given at orientation.
5. Tap the image upload field and choose your photo from your camera roll. You can take a new photo directly from the form if you prefer.
6. Complete your caption - the sentence on the form will give you a starting point. Just finish the sentence in your own words.
7. Tap Submit.
8. You'll see a confirmation screen. Your image has been received and will be reviewed before it goes into the exhibition.

**What if I don't have a smartphone?**

Tell your facilitator. They can submit the image on your behalf using their own phone, with your participant code and your caption. You choose the image - they just do the sending.

**What if I change my mind about an image after I've submitted it?**

Contact your facilitator as soon as possible. Images are reviewed before they go into the exhibition, so if you let your facilitator know quickly there is usually time to swap or remove an image. Once an image has been printed and installed in the exhibition it can still be removed, but this will leave a gap. You always have the right to remove your image - just tell your facilitator.

**A reminder about public display**

The image you submit will be printed and displayed in a public place where anyone can see it, including people with phone cameras. Once it is on the wall it may be photographed and shared on social media by members of the public. Please only submit images you are comfortable with strangers seeing and sharing. If you are unsure, talk to your facilitator before submitting.

---

#### What the Form Captures

The participant submission form captures:

- Site code - filled in automatically from the QR code link, not visible to the participant
- Week number - filled in automatically, shown on the form for participant verification
- Cohort - filled in automatically from the QR code link
- Participant code - entered by the participant
- The image file - uploaded from camera roll or taken directly
- The participant's caption - one sentence stem completed in the participant's own words

The form does not capture the participant's name, phone number, location data, or any other identifying information beyond the participant code.

#### For Facilitators

**Generating the weekly QR codes**

Each week's QR code encodes a Wordpress Form URL with site code, week number, and cohort pre-filled as hidden fields. You will receive a set of eight QR code cards for each cohort at site registration - one card per week per cohort, sixteen cards in total. Print them before Week 1 and keep them in order.

If a card is lost or damaged, the project coordinator can regenerate it. The QR code generation instructions are also in the GitHub repository if you need to generate one yourself.

**The submission window**

Each weekly QR code is active from the end of the session until 48 hours before the next session. The form page checks the current date against your site's session schedule and displays a notice if a submission is outside the active window. Late submissions are still accepted but may not make it into that week's physical exhibition - the project coordinator will contact you if a reprint is needed.

**What happens after submission**

Images go directly to the project's secure submissions storage. They are not publicly visible until the project coordinator has reviewed them. The coordinator aims to complete moderation within 24 hours of your session day and will email you the print-ready files once approved.

You do not need to collect or forward images yourself. Your only image-related task is showing participants the weekly QR code card and ensuring anyone without a smartphone can submit via the fallback process.

**The fallback submission process**

If a participant cannot submit their own image - no smartphone, no confidence with the form, or connectivity problems - photograph their chosen image with your own phone and complete the form on their behalf using their participant code and caption as they dictate it. Note the fallback submission in your session notes form.

See the Exhibition Guide (Section 2.5) for print size, paper, and source guidance.
